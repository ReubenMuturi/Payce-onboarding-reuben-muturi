Overall Impression
The current UI is clean, modern, and functional — good foundation for an early-stage product. The purple branding is strong, and the layout is mobile-friendly. However, it still feels a bit basic and transactional. We can make it more delightful, persuasive, and revenue-generating.

Key UI/UX Strengths

Clean typography and good use of white space.
Clear pricing in OMR.
Good categorization (Sauce, Fries, etc.).
QR code for sharing is excellent.
Split payment options are well presented.


Areas for Improvement
1. Visual Hierarchy & Polish

The menu items feel a bit flat. Add subtle shadows, better image cropping, and hover/tap feedback.
Category tabs could be more prominent (bigger, with icons).
Use consistent card styling across the app.

2. Information Architecture

The “Payment Progress” bar is good, but it should show who has paid what in group orders.
Pickup details are buried — should be more visible.

3. Conversion & Trust

Missing trust signals (e.g., “Secured by Amwal Pay”, lock icon, testimonials).
No urgency or social proof (“Popular choice”, “Best seller”).


Upselling Techniques to Implement
Here are high-impact upselling ideas you can add:
1. Smart Item Recommendations (Highest Priority)

When viewing an item, show “Frequently bought together” or “Customers also ordered”.
Example: Next to “Krave Double Burger” → show “+ House Sauce” and “+ Krave Kajun Fries” as quick-add buttons.

2. Bundle Deals

Create “Ramadan Box” bundles with discount.
Show “Save 0.500 OMR” when adding sauce + fries.

3. Strategic Placement

After adding items to the bill, show a “Recommended for you” section at the bottom of the bill before payment.
In the payment screen, show “Add a drink for only 0.400” as a last-mile upsell.
